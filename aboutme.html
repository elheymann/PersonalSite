Career Story

    As a child, I was always fascinated with computers. My most memorable moment was when I was in middle school in the 90s. I had the opportunity to learn a programming language called Logo where I moved a turtle to draw geometric shapes.  It was like a puzzle to me and I loved the sense of fulfillment with solving it. From then on, I was always working with computers or helping someone work on computer projects or technical issues. In college, I was exposed to a few coding challenges and wanted to pursue a degree in IT. After 9/11, I kept feeling a sense of obligation to serve my country as many of my family members did before me and gain IT experience while doing so. Although it opened many doors when I became a government contractor, I did not like the atmosphere I was in. So I decided to step away and became a stay at home mom with my young children while attaining my MBA. I missed being in the technical world, but I did not want to continue down the same path. I wanted to go back to what I enjoyed - coding. I contemplated a few coding schools, and finally chose Codeup.

   The atmosphere is friendlier and welcoming than what I have been accustomed to, and with my personality I definitely enjoy it. I love how there are so many things to learn and that we can challenge ourselves to find innovative ways to accomplish the same task. After Codeup, I want to gain experience in the field by finding a web development position while continuing to learn more about new technologies and/or languages to expand my knowledge. My “end goal”, after I have a few years of coding in the workplace under my belt, would be to continue coding. Much like the calling I had to serve my country, one day I hope to be an advocate for women in technology.
